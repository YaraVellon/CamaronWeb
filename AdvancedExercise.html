WHY SHRIMPS?

Shrimps are small, decapod crustaceans found in both freshwater and saltwater environments worldwide. They have elongated bodies, a semi-transparent exoskeleton, and ten legs, with the front ones often having small claws. Their antennae are long and sensitive, helping them navigate their surroundings.
